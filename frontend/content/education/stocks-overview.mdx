A <GlossaryTooltip term="Stock" definition="A type of security that represents ownership in a corporation." /> represents a small slice of a company. When you buy a share, you become a _shareholder_—an owner!

Owning stocks can yield returns through _capital appreciation_ and _dividends_.

> "In the short run, the market is a voting machine, but in the long run, it is a weighing machine." — **Benjamin Graham**

### Key Takeaways

1. Stocks represent **ownership**, not a loan.
2. Prices fluctuate based on **supply and demand**.
3. Long-term investing leverages **compound growth**.
